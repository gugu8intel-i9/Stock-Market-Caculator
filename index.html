Understanding Stock Market Metrics

P/E Ratio

Measures how much investors are willing to pay per dollar of earnings. A high P/E might indicate overvaluation or high growth expectations, while a low P/E could signal undervaluation or problems.

Dividend Yield

Shows the percentage return a company pays out to shareholders in dividends. High yields can indicate income stability but may also suggest limited growth opportunities.

Earnings Per Share (EPS)

Represents a company's profit allocated to each outstanding share. Growing EPS is generally positive, but should be analyzed alongside revenue growth and industry context.

Book Value Per Share

Indicates the net asset value per share. Comparing this to the market price gives the price-to-book ratio. Values below book value may indicate undervaluation.
